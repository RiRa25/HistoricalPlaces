Historical Places in India

Taj Mahal - Agra

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India.
It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal
and is considered one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture.
The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's cultural heritage.

Ellora Caves - Aurangabad

Ellora Caves is a UNESCO World Heritage site located in Maharashtra, India.
The site contains 34 rock-cut caves that were built between the 6th and 10th centuries.
The caves are a blend of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain monuments and feature intricate carvings and sculptures.
The most famous cave at Ellora is the Kailasa Temple, which is considered to be one of the largest monolithic structures in the world.

Virupaksha Temple - Hampi

The Virupaksha Temple is a Hindu temple located in the city of Hampi, in the southern state of Karnataka, India.
The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and has been in continuous use since the 7th century AD.
It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the oldest functioning temples in India. The temple complex has numerous shrines and towers, with the main tower reaching a height of 50 meters.

Sun Temple - Konark

The Konark Temple, also known as the Sun Temple, is a Hindu temple located in the state of Odisha, India.
The temple is dedicated to the Hindu Sun God Surya and was built in the 13th century.
It is designed in the shape of a colossal chariot with intricate carvings and sculptures.
The temple is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and is considered one of the finest examples of Kalinga architecture.
